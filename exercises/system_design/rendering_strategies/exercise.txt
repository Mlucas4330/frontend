Correction: 

On the bonus — you got the core idea right, but let me sharpen it:
The real trade-off is latency vs infrastructure cost.
SSG+ISR serves pre-built HTML from a CDN, so response time is ~50ms anywhere in the world. The downside is stale content — a reader might see an article for up to N seconds/minutes after it was updated, depending on your revalidation window.
SSR always serves fresh content but every request hits your server, response time is ~200-500ms, and under high traffic you pay for that compute. Caching helps, but you're right that once the TTL expires every request is a full server render again.
For a blog updated a few times per week, the staleness window of ISR is completely acceptable. SSR would be overkill — you'd be paying for real-time freshness on content that doesn't change in real-time.
